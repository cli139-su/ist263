Lion Head Meatballs

Growing up, I’ve only had this dish a couple of times. However, recently, I was craving it while it was snowing so I made it. Originally, this dish is supposed to be in meatball form and deep fried. However, I took on a time and health approach, so I made the meatballs into patties and pan-fried them instead in their own juices. I also skipped a lot of steps to make this faster. OMG they were so good! I froze the leftovers in Ziploc bags and they reheated great in the microwave—perfect for weekday meal prep!

The inspiration for this recipe came from The Woks of Life—check out their version here!

*Note: I don’t measure ingredients. This is intuitive Asian cooking—adjust to your taste!*

Ingredients:

1 lb of ground pork
A thumb of ginger
1 egg
1 scallion
1 spoon of cooking wine (like Shaoxing wine, or mirin)
½ spoon white pepper powder
½ spoon five spice powder
2 spoons corn starch
1 can water chestnuts

Time:

30 minutes

Instructions:

Mince ginger and scallion.
Dice drained water chestnuts.
Combine everything in a bowl.
Form into croquette-sized patties.
Pan-fry in a bit of oil until golden brown on both sides.
Serve with white rice and steamed bok choy!
