Lions Head Meatballs

Growing up, I’ve only had this dish a couple of times. However, recently, I was craving it while it was snowing so I made it. Originally, this dish is supposed to be in meatball form and deep fried. However, I took on a time and health approach, so I made the meatballs into patties and pan fried it instead in its own juices. I also skipped a lot of steps to make this faster. OMG they were so good! I freezed the leftovers in Ziploc bags and they turned out good even when it was reheated in the microwave, so you should definitely meal prep this dish for quick and easy weekday meals!

The inspo for this recipe was taken from The Woks of Life, so you should definitely check out that pages and browse through their recipes!

You can check out their recipe here!

*I also wanted to point out that the ingredients are not measured. This recipe is purely Asian cooking so adjust according to your tastes and wishes.*

Ingredients:

1 lb of ground pork
A thumb of ginger
1 egg
1 Scallion
1 spoon of cooking wine (like Shaoxing wine, I used mirin in my batch and it was fine)
½ spoon full of white pepper powder
½ spoon of five spice powder
2 spoons of corn starch
1 can of water chestnuts

Time:

30 minutes

Instructions

Make the patties
Mince the ginger finely Drain the can of water chestnuts and dice the water chestnuts
Mince the scallion
Combine everything to a bowl In a pan, add a small amount of oil (the fat from the patties will help coat the pan later on).
Pan fry croquette sized patties in the pan until both sides are golden brown.
Enjoy over some white rice and steamed bok choy!
